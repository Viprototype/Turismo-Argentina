Reserva Ecológica, Costanera Sur

Declared a natural park and Ecological Reserve Zone in 1986, this site is home to over 2000 animal species, including birds, mammals, amphibians, and reptiles. It boasts native vegetation such as its cortaderas grasslands and alder forests. With viewpoints overlooking the Río de la Plata, it is an ideal location for hiking and birdwatching.

Puente de la Mujer, Puerto Madero

Inaugurated in December 2001, manufactured in Spain as Argentina doesn't produce its steel, this bridge features a rotating mechanism operated by a computer system located on its eastern side, allowing the passage of boats. The design of this pedestrian bridge is a synthesis of the image of a couple dancing tango.

Avenida Alvear, Recoleta

Stretching over 7 blocks, Avenida Alvear is renowned for its buildings of rich architecture, which have been converted into embassies, hotels, exclusive clubs, or government buildings. It is an Area of Historical Protection and has been ranked by NBC among the top five in the world for its combination of quality, glamour, and price in its businesses.

Plaza de las Naciones Unidas, Recoleta

This park, with its extensive grounds and spacious green areas, features at its center the sculpture Floralis Genérica, a gift from Argentine architect Eduardo Catalano, made with materials provided by the aircraft company Lockheed Martin Aircraft Argentina. It represents a large flower made of stainless steel, with an aluminum and reinforced concrete skeleton, facing towards the sky, extending its six petals towards it. It weighs 18 tons and stands 23 meters tall.

El Rosedal, Palermo

This park has been declared a Historic Heritage site, boasting a wide variety of roses. In 2012 and 2014, it received the most prestigious international distinction awarded to rose gardens, recognizing its beauty, history, and educational value: the Garden Excellence Award. This award is presented annually by the World Federation of Rose Societies (WFRS).
